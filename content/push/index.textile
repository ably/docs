A push notification is a message that appears on a device, similar to a text message or pop-up. It's a way for an app to send information or alerts to its users even when the app is not actively being used. These notifications can include reminders, updates, or other relevant messages to engage users or provide timely information.

Ably delivers push notifications using:

- "Apple Push Notifications (APNs)":https://developer.apple.com/notifications/ := for iOS devices.
- "Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)":https://firebase.google.com/docs/cloud-messaging/ := for Android devices.

h2. Push notification process

The following is an overview of the push notification process:

h3. Setup

h4. Setup for APNs

* Create an APNs .p12 Certificate for your App. Obtain a certificate from APNs to enable push notifications for your iOS app.
* Register your app with APNs. Ensure your app is registered with APNs to send and receive notifications.
* Implement delegate methods. In your app's @UIApplicationDelegate@, implement the necessary delegate methods to handle token registration and manage push notifications.

h4. Setup for FCM

* Create a Firebase Project. Set up a project in Firebase to manage your Android app's notifications.
* Add your Android App to Firebase. Configure your Android app within the Firebase project to use Firebase services.
* Create a Service Account. Generate a service account in Firebase for authentication and authorization purposes.
* Implement a service extending @FirebaseMessagingService@. Develop a service in your Android app to handle the receipt of incoming push notifications through FCM.

h3. Device activation

* Register devices with APNs or FCM. Ensure devices are registered with either APNs or FCM to receive notifications.
* Use "Ably’s client libraries":/docs/api/realtime-sdk/push-admin?lang=javascript. Ably’s client libraries offer a consistent API across all platforms for device registration and for managing the receipt of push notifications.

h3. Subscribe

* Subscribe devices directly using "deviceID":push/setup/#subscribing-device-id. Enable devices to receive push notifications by subscribing directly using their unique deviceID.
* Subscribe devices through channels using "clientID":push/setup/#subscribing-client-id. Alternatively, subscribe devices to notifications through channels, utilizing a clientID for identification.

h3. Publish push notifications

h4. Direct publish 

* Enable direct publishing to devices for instant content updates.
* Utilize unique deviceIDs for targeted notifications to specific devices.

h4. Channel-based publish

* Enable channel-based publishing for wider distribution.
* Send notifications through predefined channels to reach subscribed devices.

Ably provides two methods for delivering push notifications to devices:

h3(#direct). Direct publishing

"Directly publishing":/publish#direct-publishing push notifications to devices enables personalized communication via alerts and messages. Devices must register with an APN or FCM and obtain a unique identifier — the application stores this identifier, linking it to user accounts or device profiles. Upon an event triggering a notification, the application’s backend constructs the message. The backend sends it directly to the specific device using the identifier via the push notification service. This service then ensures the device receives the notification, displays it to the user, or allows the app to handle it.

h3(#channel-based). Channel-based publishing

"Channel-based publishing":/publish#channel-publishing uses "Pub/Sub Channels":/channels to enable applications' realtime push notification delivery. The process starts with creating a channel for devices to subscribe to and then registering templates with a push notification service to receive a unique identifier. When events trigger notifications, they are published to the channel and automatically sent to all subscribed devices via push notification services, APNs, or FCM. The messaging service handles delivery, ensuring notifications are correctly formatted and reach devices where users see them or the application manages them if active.

h2. Manage push notifications

Ably manages push notifications by handling device activations through the client or server, direct publishing to devices, and publishing notifications through channels. It also includes managing device registrations and channel subscriptions through the Push Admin API, offering a comprehensive toolkit for delivering notifications efficiently and monitoring their performance across platforms You can manage your push notifcations using push notifcation management or channel-based push notification management.

h3. Direct push notifcation management

Direct push notifcation management involves individual devices handling push notification settings and subscriptions through "Ably's client SDKs":https://ably.com/docs/sdks. Devices must register with the push notification service specific to their operating system and then activate themselves for push notifications via the Ably SDK. Devices can subscribe to their channels to receive push notifications publish over those channels. Note, direct device management limitations are device specific.

h3. Channel-based push notification management

Channel-based push notification management, also known as push Admin:, provides centralized administrative control over all aspects of push notification delivery and device management. It is designed for server use, allowing administrative actions through the "Push Admin API":/api/realtime-sdk/push-admin. This API offers complete control over the management and delivery of push notifications, including the ability to manage registered devices, their subscriptions to channels, and direct push notifications.
