The usage page is an interface to monitor your app's performance and usage via the "quota and usage statistics table":#table and "usage statistics chart":#chart.


h2(#table). Quota and usage statistics table

The quota and usage statistics table provides an overview of your account's allocation, showing how many resources (messages, data, connections, and channels) are available, helping you stay within your plan's limits:

<a href="@content/screenshots/dash/stats-table.png" target="_blank">
  <img src="@content/screenshots/dash/stats-table.png" style="width: 100%" alt="Your stats table">
</a>

The following explains how to use the statistics table:

*Track usage* _Monitor your usage of messages (published, received, history), data transferred, peak connections, and channels - in realtime._

*Analyze resource consumption* _View key metrics such as messages and data transferred to understand how your account's resources are being consumed and compare them to previous usage patterns._

*Compare usage trends* _Compare data across different periods (last month vs. this month) to identify changes in resource consumption._

*Review comments* _Pay attention to comments in the table, which provide additional insights into your usage. For example, if no messages have been sent, it may prompt you to investigate your application’s configuration._

h2(#chart). Statistics chart

The usage page also includes a chart that visualizes your app's data over time:

<a href="@content/screenshots/dash/stats-chart.png" target="_blank">
  <img src="@content/screenshots/dash/stats-chart.png" style="width: 100%" alt="Your stats chart">
</a>

The following explains how to use the statistics chart:

*Duration* _Define a specific time range for the statistics you want to view. This enables you to focus on periods of particular interest. For example, set the time range from "2024-06-17 00:00" to "2024-08-06 11:18" to analyze data within that period._

*Zoom* _Use preset zoom options (1h, 8h, 24h, 7d, 1m, 6m, 1y, all) to adjust the chart's view to different periods, enabling you to analyze data at various granularities._

<aside data-type='note'>
<p>The shaded areas visually indicate the range of activity, highlighting periods of variability and stability within the metrics.</p>
</aside>
