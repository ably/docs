Ably's platform architecture is built to deliver dependable realtime experiences at global scale. As the definitive realtime experience platform, we serve billions of devices monthly, powering critical applications for businesses like HubSpot, NASCAR, and Webflow with unmatched reliability and performance.

h3. At a Glance 

Our multi-region infrastructure forms the foundation of our platform, allowing us to maintain exceptional performance while serving massive scale. This globally distributed architecture doesn't just provide redundancy - it ensures that even during regional failures or network partitions, your realtime applications continue to function seamlessly.

We characterise our system across "4 pillars of dependability":https://ably.com/four-pillars-of-dependability :

* **Performance**: We focus on predictability of latencies to provide certainty in uncertain operating conditions.
** <50ms global median latency
** 19M+ concurrent connections
* **Reliability**: Ably is designed to preserve continuity of service we ensure sufficient redundancy at a regional and global level.
** 5+ years since last outage
** 99.999999% message survivability
* **Scale**: Our service is elastic, highly-available and built to handle extreme scale and large numbers of concurrent connections.
** 1B+ connected devices per month
** 2T+ API operations per month
* **Network**: We operate a truly distributed global edge network to delivers a globally consistent experience to users.
** 635 points of presence
** 11 globally distributed regions

h3. Design Objectives

Ably's platform is a global service that supports all of our realtime messaging and associated services. It is architected to achieve horizontal scalability with no effective ceiling on application scale, while maintaining consistent latency, message integrity, and system reliability across our global network.

The platform has been designed with the following primary objectives in mind:

* **Horizontal scalability**: The platform must achieve Internet-scale horizontally; as more nodes are added, the load is automatically redistributed across the cluster so that global capacity increases linearly with the number of instances we run.
* **No single point of congestion**: This is closely related to horizontal scalability but is an objective in its own right. As the system scales, there is no single point of congestion for any data path, and data within the system is routed peer-to-peer, ensuring no single component becomes overloaded as traffic scales for an individual app or across the cluster.
* **Fault tolerance**: Faults in the system are expected, and the system must have redundancy at every layer in the stack to ensure availability (readiness to provide service) and reliability (continuity across failure events). Distributed systems are "designed for failure."
* **Autonomy**: Each component in the system should be able to operate fully without reliance on a global controller. For example, two isolated data centers should continue to service realtime requests while isolated.
* **Consistent low latencies**: Within data centers, we aim for latencies to be in the low 10s of milliseconds and less than 100ms globally. Consistently achieving low latencies requires careful consideration of the placement of data and processing roles across the system, as well as the prioritization of work within each role.
* **Quality of service**: We intentionally set higher QoS targets than our competitors because we believe in the opportunities to build services on top of this platform. Without ordering, delivery guarantees, and deterministic outcomes, the scope of services that can be built on top of the platform becomes limited.
