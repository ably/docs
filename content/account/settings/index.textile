h2. Single sign-on (SSO)

Set up SSO for your Ably account using any SAML-compatible identity provider:

* SSO is available to *enterprise* customers and must be enabled per account by contacting Ably support.
* Only account owners can configure SSO settings.
* Step-by-step examples are provided for setting up SSO, including necessary configurations in both the Ably dashboard and the identity provider's platform.

See: "Single sign-on (SSO).":/account/settings/sso

<aside data-type='note'>
<p>User provisioning and deprovisioning via SCIM is not supported at Account user level permission.</p>
</aside>

h2. Two-factor authentication (2FA)

Enhance the security of your Ably account, adding an extra layer of protection by requiring a secondary verification method alongside your password:

* 2FA requires both your password and a verification code from your mobile device or authenticator app, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.
* Choose between SMS verification or Time-Based One-Time Passwords (TOTP) via authenticator apps like Authy or Google Authenticator.

See: "Two-factor authentication (2FA).":/account/settings/2fa

h2. Account billing

Manage your billing and package settings to scale your services:


* Review your current plan to assess if it fits your needs, whether free, pay-as-you-go, or committed use.
* Ensure your billing information is up to date, including company name, billing email (optional), address, international phone number, and timezone.
* View and manage invoices and payment methods once your account has been upgraded.

See: "Account billing.":/account/settings/billing

h2. Subscription limits

Understand and manage your subscription plan by tracking usage across messages, data, connections, and channels:

* Limits are applied to prevent service disruption and vary by package.
* Limits can be time-based, quantity-based, or instantaneous rates and are categorized as local or global.
* Soft limits act as thresholds where overages may incur charges without restrictions, while hard limits enforce restrictions when exceeded.
* Notifications are provided for global limits when thresholds are nearing or exceeded, and detailed logs of warnings and exceeded limits are accessible in the account dashboard.

See: "Limits.":/general/limits

h2. Account usage

Monitor your account's resource consumption with detailed usage statistics:

* The quota and usage statistics table monitors app performance by tracking resource usage, such as messages, data transferred, connections, and channels.
* The statistics chart visualizes app usage data over time, allowing users to define specific time ranges, zoom in on different periods, and analyze metrics in detail.

See: "Account usage.":/account/settings/usage

h2. User management

Manage the users associated with your account:

* The account owner role has full permissions to manage the account, including inviting and removing users, and assigning roles like *developer*, *billing*, or *admin*.
* Multiple roles can be assigned to a single user.
* Remove, or change user roles, as well as how to delete an Ably account.

See: "User management.":/account/settings/users

h2. Personal account settings

The *my settings* tab enables Ably users to control personal account settings:

* View and edit name, email, and password.
* Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.
* Connect and manage login providers (Google, GitHub).
* Customize Ably email preferences (product updates, news, educational emails)
* Set up notifications for account usage, including quota limits and service impact alerts.

h2. Token management

Create and manage access tokens for the control API:

* Users can create new tokens by providing a descriptive name, assigning an account, selecting capabilities, and securely storing the token once generated.
* Users can revoke existing tokens through the settings section, but this action is irreversible and will immediately invalidate the token for API access.
* After a token is created, the full token cannot be viewed again, so it must be securely stored upon generation.

See: "Token management.":/account/settings/token
